abstract: >-
  The development of artificial intelligence technology and the expansion of its scope of application have raised user experiences, perceptions, and concerns about artificial intelligence technology-based content works, and the importance of discussions on them is increasing. Based on this background, this study examined whether there are differnces in perception depending on their expertise or experience in artificial intelligence, whether there are any changes in perception, and what are the potential legal and ethical issues that experts and users perceive. To this end, a total of 11 people, including industry experts, academic experts, and general users, were interviewed in writing through structured questionnaires containing visual aids. As a result of the study, there were differences in perception depending on the expertise in artificial intelligence, and changes in perception occurred depending on the experience of use. We also derive potential legal and ethical issues in the creation and utilization of content works generated by artificial intelligence technology. Through this study, we explored various user perceptions related to the creation and utilization of artificial intelligence technology-based content works, and present implications for the use of desirable artificial intelligence technologies in the field of content creation, including culture and art.
