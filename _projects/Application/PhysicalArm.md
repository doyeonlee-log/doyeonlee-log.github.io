    <h5 class="card-title fw-bold">Cognitive-Inspired Robotics</h5>
    <p class="card-text text-secondary">
      This research integrates the concept of 'Metacognition (the human ability to monitor and regulate one's own cognitive processes)' into the control system of a physical robotic arm. Unlike conventional fixed scanning methods, the Metacognition-based Adaptive Scanning System (MASS) enables the robot to autonomously monitor and evaluate its scanning state and the uncertainty of acquired data. Based on this self-assessment, the system dynamically adjusts scanning paths, speed, and precision in real-time. This project aims to develop and implement algorithms that achieve high-quality 3D data acquisition with optimal efficiency, even within complex and unpredictable environments.
